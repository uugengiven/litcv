Michael Bofshever

Father Daniel

Michael Bofshever has sustained a distinguished acting career for over forty years. He made his stage debut in Al Pacino’s Richard III for the Theatre Company of Boston and went on to be a founding member of The Actors Producing Company in New York City. His stage career has included The Actors Studio, Circle Repertory Company, The Ensemble Studio Theatre, The Taper Too Festival and West Coast Jewish Theater, to name only a few.

Michael has an extensive list of guest starring TV roles, most recently on How To Get Away With Murder, Barry, Scandal, True Blood, The Shield, Private Practice and dozens more. He is perhaps best known for his recurring role as Mr. Pinkman on Breaking Bad, as well as a series regular on the Disney Channel’s The Jersey in the role of Larry Lighter. His many film credits include, Lasse Hallstorm’s A Dog’s Purpose, Paul Greengrass’s United 93, Mark Rydell’s Crime of the Century, R.J. Daniel Hanna’s Miss Virginia, Vince Gilligan’s El Camino: A Breaking Bad Movie.

Michael made his directorial and co-writing debut, with his award winning short film, Echo Park Blues. The film was voted best short at The New York Jazz Film Festival, Film Miami Shorts Fest, Culver City Film Festival, St. Lawrence International Film Festival; and the Best Independents International Film Festival where he was honored with Best Director along with Honorable Mention at the Roma Cinema Doc Fest. Laurel wins and nominations were awarded to his actors, editor and cinematographer.

Author of Your Face Looks Familiar…How To Get Ahead as a Working Actor, Michael has taught acting privately for twenty years, USC School of Cinematic Arts, Chapman University and UCLA Extension. He has spoken on the topic of how to create and maintain an acting career, Journey of the Working Actor, sponsored by the Screen Actors Guild Foundation, at university theater and film programs nationwide. He was profiled in the “Actors Guide to Qualified Acting Coaches-Los Angeles.” Michael has moderated discussions for the Screen Actors Guild and major movie studios on the topic of career and craft, with luminary actors, writers and directors in the entertainment field.
