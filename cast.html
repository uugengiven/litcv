Angela Gulner

Luna

Angela Gulner is an actor, writer, and educator with her MFA in Acting from Harvard University.

She's best known for co-creating and starring in hit indie web series BINGE, and has been seen on GLOW, SINGLE PARENTS, SILICON VALLEY, and STALKER in addition to on stages all over the world.

Ian Remmers

Leaf

Ian comes from the rural Finger Lakes region of upstate New York where he was raised to believe in the importance of community and story telling. This was the foundation for his love and pursuit of acting.

Having spent two years training at the Actors Workshop of Ithaca, Ian was able grow as a performer through work with local theatre companies before completing his Bachelors degree at SUNY Purchase this past May. He is excited to be apart of this series as we all learn to navigate storytelling in the new normal of 2020.

Katie Higgins

Darcy

In 1993 Kate Higgins packed her belongings and headed to Los Angeles to make a career in music and somewhere along the way happily fell into voiceover work. For over 20 years she has given voice to a multitude of characters including work for Fisher Price, Nickelodeon, & Mattel.

She has voiced roles in hundreds of anime series and video games as well as commercials and promos for CBS, ABC, and the Disney Channel. If you need a consummate professional with a wide range of voice styles, she can be contacted through the William Morris Endeavor Entertainment agency in Beverly Hills CA. Her albums can be found on iTunes, Amazon, and CD Baby.

Kate is excited to be involved in this exciting and groundbreaking project now as an on camera actress. She has known Steve Armour, the writer, since she was a child as they grew up in the same town and performed in jazz bands with him on many occasions. To have had a part written specifically for her is a true delight and she is honored and grateful to be playing Darcy.

Michael Bofshever

Father Daniel

Michael Bofshever has sustained a distinguished acting career for over forty years. He made his stage debut in Al Pacino’s Richard III for the Theatre Company of Boston and went on to be a founding member of The Actors Producing Company in New York City. His stage career has included The Actors Studio, Circle Repertory Company, The Ensemble Studio Theatre, The Taper Too Festival and West Coast Jewish Theater, to name only a few.

Michael has an extensive list of guest starring TV roles, most recently on How To Get Away With Murder, Barry, Scandal, True Blood, The Shield, Private Practice and dozens more. He is perhaps best known for his recurring role as Mr. Pinkman on Breaking Bad, as well as a series regular on the Disney Channel’s The Jersey in the role of Larry Lighter. His many film credits include, Lasse Hallstorm’s A Dog’s Purpose, Paul Greengrass’s United 93, Mark Rydell’s Crime of the Century, R.J. Daniel Hanna’s Miss Virginia, Vince Gilligan’s El Camino: A Breaking Bad Movie.

Michael made his directorial and co-writing debut, with his award winning short film, Echo Park Blues. The film was voted best short at The New York Jazz Film Festival, Film Miami Shorts Fest, Culver City Film Festival, St. Lawrence International Film Festival; and the Best Independents International Film Festival where he was honored with Best Director along with Honorable Mention at the Roma Cinema Doc Fest. Laurel wins and nominations were awarded to his actors, editor and cinematographer.

Author of Your Face Looks Familiar…How To Get Ahead as a Working Actor, Michael has taught acting privately for twenty years, USC School of Cinematic Arts, Chapman University and UCLA Extension. He has spoken on the topic of how to create and maintain an acting career, Journey of the Working Actor, sponsored by the Screen Actors Guild Foundation, at university theater and film programs nationwide. He was profiled in the “Actors Guide to Qualified Acting Coaches-Los Angeles.” Michael has moderated discussions for the Screen Actors Guild and major movie studios on the topic of career and craft, with luminary actors, writers and directors in the entertainment field. To learn more go to: michaelbofshever.com

Yohance Myles

Jamal

YOHANCE MYLES is a native of Birmingham, AL. He received his BA in Theatre from Alabama State University, and MFA in Acting from LSU. Yohance is a(n) member of AEA (Equity) and SAG-AFTRA (film/televison).

Yohance Myles can be seen starring as “Councilman Kent Hamilton” on OWN’s Network hit drama TV series “Ambitions” produced by Will Packer, Oprah Winfrey, and Lionsgate Studio. He has successfully been accredited with working for several major TV networks such as; FOX, OWN, NBC, ABC, CW, AMC, and the Lifetime Network to name a few.

Myles has produced dynamic performances in recurring roles on major TV series such as; Containment, Into the Badlands, and The Originals.” He has also guest-starred on hit shows including: “NCIS: New Orleans, Blue Bloods to name a few. Things aren’t slowing down for Myles either. He can be recently seen in the feature film “The Secret: Dare to Dream” starring Josh Lucas and Katie Holmes.

Myles is a member of Alpha Phi AlphaFraternity Inc. In addition, Myles serves as National spokesperson for the SCL Foundation,which was founded by iconic actors’ Sidney Poitier and Harry Belafonte.

Yohance Myles is the director of “The BEing Within Actor’s” Studio Session,which guides inspiring actors with knowledge and life skills to sustain themselves and their talent(s) in the competitively growing entertainment and media industry. He believes the impact of his success and teaching(s) continues to serve the lives of many inspiring artist/actors across the Southeast.

Myles enjoys spending time with his wife and children. His hobbies include a lot reading, gym training, and assisting others in their creative efforts towards success.

John Newberg

Carson

John Newberg has appeared in over 40 Film and Television productions. You may have seen him in THE BLIND SIDE, THE RESIDENT, or DARK WATERS, just to name a few. He’s worked opposite Jon Voight, Mark Ruffalo, Greg Kinnear, Woody Harrelson, James Marsden, and many others.

He made his LA theatrical debut in the role of Billy Bob in LUANN HAMPTON LAVERTY OBERLANDER at the Pasadena Playhouse. John studied at the Acting Studio in Nashville, the American Academy of Dramatic Arts in Pasadena, and privately with David LeGrant, Michael Keenan, Margie Haber, and Shari Shaw. He’s thrilled to be part of LOVE IN THE TIME OF CORONAVIRUS.
